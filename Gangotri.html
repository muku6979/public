Top Places To Visit In Gangotri



  1. Gangotri Temple


    2. Visitor Information

    • Famous for: Pilgrimage.
    • Tickets: No entry fee
    • Opening Timings:All Days – 6.15 AM to 2 PM and 3 PM – 9.30 PM
    • Duration: 1.5-2 hours.

    About Gangotri Temple

    The Gangotri temple is an impressive structure built with marble that soars 20 feet high, and is touted to be over 300 years old. This beautiful temple is dedicated to Goddess Ganga, and was erected in the 18th century by Amar Singh Thapa – a Gurkha Commander. The Maharaja of Jaipur rebuilt this temple in the 20th century, and over 2.5 lakh pilgrims flock this temple every year. When in Gangotri, be sure to visit the temple and be a part of the impressive Aarti followed by the Ganga Aarti performed to thank the river goddess.


  2. Pandav Gufa


    3. Visitor Information

    • Famous for: ancient natural cave.
    • Tickets:No entry fee.
    • Opening Timings: Open all days at all times.
    • Duration:2-3 hours

    About Pandav Gufa

    Pandav Gufa is an ancient natural cave, located 1.5 km from Gangotri Dham. The cave is reached via a short trek. Pandav Gufa is believed to be the place where Pandavs from Mahabharat meditated on their way to Mount Kailash. The trekking trail has lush greenery and scenic views all along. The trek to Pandav Gufa starts from GMVN Guesthouse located near Suraj Kund Waterfall. The route isn't marked, so try to reach back before dark. A lone sadhu lives in the Pandav Gufa.


  3. Gomukh


    4. Visitor Information

    • Famous for:Ganges River
    • Tickets:No entry fee.
    • Opening Timings: Open all days.
    • Duration: 1-2 hours.

    About Gomukh

    Gomukh is the source of the Bhagirathi River in Gangotri Glacier, one of the primary headstreams of the Ganges River. The place is situated at a height of 13,200 ft (4,023 m) in Uttarkashi district.


  4. KedarKantha trek


    5. Visitor Information

    • Famous for:Nature, Trekking.
    • Tickets:Not Specified
    • Opening Timings:Open all days in November to April
    • Duration: 3 hours.

    About KedarKantha trek

    Kedarkantha is such a popular winter trek that it would be apt to entitle it as the Queen of Winter Treks. Knee-deep snow in winters, mesmerizing view from the top, and the beautiful and easy trails make it popular among trekkers. The ‘easy on endurance’ trail goes through the thick pine forests of Govind National Park. The views from the top are worth climbing to an altitude of 12,500 ft.
